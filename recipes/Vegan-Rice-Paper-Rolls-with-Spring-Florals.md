title: "Vegan Rice Paper Rolls with Spring Florals"
status: published
date: 2018-10-10
description: "A simple, tasty, healthy recipe using our Rice Vermicelli Noodles and Hoisin, Soy, and Sesame oil sauces. Beautiful and really speaks to the season!"
permalink: "/recipes/Vegan-Rice-Paper-Rolls-with-Spring-Florals/"
image: "/static/img/recipes/Vegan-Rice-Paper-Rolls-with-Spring-Florals/Vegan-Rice-Paper-Rolls-with-Spring-Florals.jpg"
makes: "20"
author: "pwg"
ingredients: |
  <h3>Ingredients:</h3>
  <ul>
  <li>250g <a href="/products/Changs-Vermicelli-Rice-Noodles/" title="Chang's Vermicelli Rice Noodles" alt="Chang's Vermicelli Rice Noodles">Chang's Vermicelli Rice Noodles</a></li>
  <li>20x edible rice paper rounds</li>
  <li>40 edible flowers and nasturtium leaves</li>
  <li>1 telegraph cucumber cut into matchsticks</li>
  <li>200g firm tofu or tempeh, sliced into matchsticks</li>
  <li>3 green onions, sliced thinly</li>
  <li>1 cup combined mint and coriander leaves</li>
  <li>10 green oak lettuce leaves, halved</li>
  <li>1/2 cup <a href="/products/Changs-Original-Hoisin-Sauce/" title="Chang's Original Hoisin Sauce" alt="Chang's Original Hoisin Sauce">Chang's Original Hoisin Sauce</a></li>
  <li>1/4 cup roasted peanuts, crushed</li>
  <li>2tbsps <a href="/products/Changs-Tamari-Light-Soy-Sauce/" title="Chang's Gluten Free Tamari Light Soy Sauce" alt="Chang's Gluten Free Tamari Light Soy Sauce">Chang's Gluten Free Tamari Light Soy Sauce</a></li>
  <li>1 tbsp <a href="/products/Changs-Sesame-Oil/" title="Chang's Sesame Oil" alt="Chang's Sesame Oil">Chang's Sesame Oil</a></li>
  <li>Juice of 1 lime</li>
  </ul>
method: |
  <h3>Method:</h3>
  <ol>
  <li>Place vermicelli in a large heatproof bowl, pour over enough boiling water to cover noodles and stand 4 minutes or until noodles are softened: drain and cut into rough lengths.</li>
  <li>Combine vermicelli in a large bowl with cucumber, tofu, onion, herbs and lettuce: set aside.</li>
  <li>Fill a large bowl with warm water.</li>
  <li>Using one piece of rice paper round at a time, submerge in water for 10-15 seconds to soften then transfer to clean working surface.</li>
  <li>Place two edible flowers or nasturtium leaves along the centre of the softened rice paper round and top with 1/4 cup of the noodle mixture.</li>
  <li>Fold in ends then roll rice paper to seal.</li>
  <li>Place on baking paper lined tray and cover with a clean damp tea towel and repeat with remaking rice paper rounds, flowers and noodle mixture.</li>
  <li>Combine Hoisin, Soy, sesame oil and lime juice in a small bowl and serve with rice paper rolls</li>
  </ol>
