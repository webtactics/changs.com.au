ingredients: |
  <h3>Ingredients:</h3>
  <ul>
  <li>4 thick (3-4 cm) slices meaty veal shin, more if they are on the small side</li>
  <li>2 tbsp. neutral oil</li>
  <li>1 thumb ginger, peeled and finely sliced</li>
  <li>&frac12; cup Shaosing rice wine</li>
  <li>2 tbsp. <a href="/products/Changs-Original-Oyster-Sauce/" title="Chang's Original Oyster Sauce" alt="Chang's Original Oyster Sauce">Chang's Original Oyster Sauce</a></li>
  <li>2 tbsp. <a href="/products/Changs-Tamari-Light-Soy-Sauce/" title="Chang's Gluten Free Tamari Light Soy Sauce" alt="Chang's Gluten Free Tamari Light Soy Sauce">Chang's Gluten Free Tamari Light Soy Sauce</a></li>
  <li>2 tbsp. brown sugar</li>
  <li>2 tbsp. rice wine vinegar</li>
  <li>1 tsp (more to taste) chilli bean paste or sauce/ chilli sauce</li>
  <li>6 star anise</li>
  <li>1 tsp Sichuan peppercorns, finely ground in mortar and pestle</li>
  <li>2 pieces dried tangerine or orange peel</li>
  </ul>
method: |
  <h3>Method:</h3>
  <ol>
  <li>Brown the meat well on both sides in the oil in a hot pan and transfer to a deep baking dish just big enough to fit the meat.</li>
  <li>Add all other ingredients and top with enough water to just cover the meat. Cover with foil or the lid of the dish. Place in a pre-heated 150 degree oven for two hours, until falling off the bone tender.</li>
  <li>Serve with steamed Asian greens like bok choy and steamed rice.</li>
  <li>Serves 4 as a small main course or generously as part of an Asian banquet.</li>
  </ol>
