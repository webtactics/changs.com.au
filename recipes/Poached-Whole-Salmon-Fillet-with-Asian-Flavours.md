ingredients: |
  <h3>Ingredients:</h3>
  <ul>
  <li>1 cup Shaosing rice wine</li>
  <li>1 cup chicken stock</li>
  <li>&frac12; cup <a href="/products/Changs-Tamari-Light-Soy-Sauce/" title="Chang's Gluten Free Tamari Light Soy Sauce" alt="Chang's Gluten Free Tamari Light Soy Sauce">Chang's Gluten Free Tamari Light Soy Sauce</a></li>
  <li>&frac12; cup <a href="/products/Changs-Original-Oyster-Sauce/" title="Chang's Original Oyster Sauce" alt="Chang's Original Oyster Sauce">Chang's Original Oyster Sauce</a></li>
  <li>2 tbsp. ginger, finely grated</li>
  <li>2 tbsp. palm sugar, grated or chopped</li>
  <li>4 whole star anise</li>
  <li>2 strips (2cm x 4cm) orange peel, white pith removed from back or one orange piece, dried orange or tangerine peel</li>
  <li>1 stick cinnamon</li>
  <li>&frac12; medium red chilli, sliced or finely diced</li>
  <li>1 whole salmon fillet, skin on, pin bones removed</li>
  <li>Shredded spring onions, to serve</li>
  </ul>
method: |
  <h3>Method:</h3>
  <ol>
  <li>Combine all ingredients except the salmon and spring onions and bring to the boil, then gently simmer for 10 minutes to dissolve the palm sugar and integrate the flavours.</li>
  <li>Place the salmon skin side down in a baking dish just big enough to hold it. Pour the liquid over the salmon, and cover the dish tightly with foil. Bake in a 200 degree oven for 15 minutes then remove from the oven, and allow to rest for another 10 minutes without removing the foil.</li>
  <li>Remove the foil and serve straight from the baking dish, or transfer to a serving platter, spooning cooking juices over the fish and topping with the shredded spring onion.</li>
  </ol>
