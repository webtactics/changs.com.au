ingredients: |
  <h3>Ingredients:</h3>
  <ul>
  <li>250g lean pork mince</li>
  <li>2 cloves garlic, crushed</li>
  <li>½ thumb ginger, peeled and grated</li>
  <li>1 tbsp.Shaosing rice wine</li>
  <li>1 tbsp.neutral oil</li>
  <li>3 spring onions, finely sliced, dark green tops reserved</li>
  <li>1 tbsp.chilli bean sauce</li>
  <li>1 cup Asian (plain) chicken stock</li>
  <li>1 tbsp.Chang’s Oyster Sauce</li>
  <li>1 tbsp.Chang’s Hoisin sauce</li>
  <li>1 tbsp.cornflour, dissolved in half a cup of cold water</li>
  <li>3-400g firm bean curd (tofu), cut into1cm cubes</li>
  <li>½ - 1 medium red chilli, finely sliced</li>
  </ul>
method: |
  <h3>Method:</h3>
  <ol>
  <li>Marinate the pork with the garlic, ginger and rice wine for about an hour.</li>
  <li>Heat the oil in a wok and add the pork, stirring occasionally until it has nearly all changed colour. Add the spring onions and sliced chilli and cook for another thirty seconds.</li>
  <li>Add the chilli bean sauce, Chang’s Oyster and Hoisin sauces and stir well to incorporate. Add the chicken stock and cook for a minute, stirring occasionally. Add the cornflour and stir until the sauce boils and thickens. Gently fold through the tofu until hot and transfer to a serving plate. Top with the sliced dark green spring onion and serve with boiled rice.</li>
  <li>Serves four as part of a Chinese meal/banquet.</li>
  <li>Gluten Free dependant upon the brand of chicken stock used.</li>
  </ol>
