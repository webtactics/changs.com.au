ingredients: |
  <h3>Ingredients:</h3>
  <ul>
  <li>2 tbsp. neutral oil</li>
  <li>600g lean pork belly, cut 3cm x 1 cm</li>
  <li>2 cloves garlic, sliced</li>
  <li>1 large thumb ginger, peeled and julienned (very thin matchsticks)</li>
  <li>6 shallots, peeled and sliced</li>
  <li>Up to a dozen whole bird&rsquo;s eye chillies, whole, for the brave, or one medium red chilli,</li>
  <li>sliced, for the sane</li>
  <li>&frac12; tsp. freshly ground black pepper</li>
  <li>4 tbsp. <a href="/products/Changs-Kecap-Manis-Sweet-Soy-Sauce/" title="Chang's Kecap Manis Sweet Soy Sauce" alt="Chang's Kecap Manis Sweet Soy Sauce">Chang's Kecap Manis Sweet Soy Sauce</a></li>
  <li>2 tbsp. <a href="/products/Changs-Tamari-Light-Soy-Sauce/" title="Chang's Gluten Free Tamari Light Soy Sauce" alt="Chang's Gluten Free Tamari Light Soy Sauce">Chang's Gluten Free Tamari Light Soy Sauce</a></li>
  <li>1 cup chicken stock</li>
  </ul>
method: |
  <h3>Method:</h3>
  <ol>
  <li>Place the oil in a wok and when hot add the pork stirring occasionally for a few minutes while browning.</li>
  <li>Add the garlic, ginger, shallots, chillies and black pepper and stir for another minute to soften then add both soy sauces, stirring and allowing them to coat the pork.</li>
  <li>Add the stock and reduce to a simmer for about thirty minutes until pork is tender and nearly all liquid is absorbed. You may need to add more stock or water if the liquid reduces too quickly.</li>
  <li>Serve with plenty of steamed rice and green vegetables.</li>
  </ol>
