ingredients: |
  <h3>Ingredients:</h3>
  <ul>
  <li>2 bundles of <a href="/products/Changs-Vermicelli-Rice-Noodles/" title="Chang's Vermicelli Rice Noodles" alt="Chang's Vermicelli Rice Noodles">Chang's Vermicelli Rice Noodles</a></li>
  <li>3 small truss tomatoes</li>
  <li>2 spring onions (white and green parts)</li>
  <li>1/2 tbsp of salted butter</li>
  <li>250 grams of non-lean pork mince (or a beef and pork mince blend)</li>
  <li>1 tbsp <a href="/products/Changs-Tamari-Light-Soy-Sauce/" title="Chang's Gluten Free Tamari Light Soy Sauce" alt="Chang's Gluten Free Tamari Light Soy Sauce">Chang's Gluten Free Tamari Light Soy Sauce</a></li>
  <li>1 tsp tomato paste</li>
  <li>1 tsp runny honey</li>
  <li>1 bunch of fresh coriander (chopped fine)</li>
  <li>1 small Lebanese cucumber or 1 quarter continental cucumber (seeds removed)</li>
  <li>1 spring onion (for garnish)</li>
  </ul>
method: |
  <h3>Method:</h3>
  <ol>
  <li><strong>Prepare the garnish.</strong><br /> Slice the spring onion in rounds or strips for garnish and set aside. The garnish may be soaked in ice water to remove the raw onion &lsquo;bite&rsquo; if preferred.<br /> Cut the cucumber in half, remove the seeds, shred with a box grater and squeeze out the excess water. Set aside.</li>
  <li><strong>Make the fresh tomato butter sauce.<br /></strong>Blend tomatoes and two spring onions with a pinch of salt using a countertop or immersion blender.<br />Transfer the blended tomato to a small saucepan and heat on medium until reduced by half.<br />Add cold butter and whisk vigorously with a wire whip or immersion blender until emulsified.<br />Add a small pinch of chopped coriander (optional) and set aside.<br />The sauce may be served as a brothy base or reduced over a medium-low flame for ten minutes, stirring to avoid sticking, for a thicker texture.</li>
  <li><strong>Prepare the pork sauce mixture.<br /></strong>Mix together <a href="/products/Changs-Tamari-Light-Soy-Sauce/" title="Chang's Gluten Free Tamari Light Soy Sauce" alt="Chang's Gluten Free Tamari Light Soy Sauce">Chang's Gluten Free Tamari Light Soy Sauce</a>, honey and tomato paste and set aside.</li>
  <li><strong>Cook the pork mince.<br /></strong>Brown the pork mince in a medium fry pan, breaking it down with a wooden spoon or spatula.<br />Let it cook undisturbed for 5 minutes, then mix and flip so the pan makes contact with the uncooked mince. Cook for a further two minutes.<br />This step aims for the &lsquo;Maillard reaction&rsquo;, which develops brown colour and complex flavour. Allowing the mince to come to room temperature beforehand assists browning.<br />Continue cooking until the water has &lsquo;burned off&rsquo; and darker charred parts appear.<br />Add the prepared sauce mixture and stir through the mince. Cook for 5 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly and caramelises.<br />Add a pinch of coriander (optional).</li>
  <li><strong>Cook the noodles.<br /></strong>Cook <a href="/products/Changs-Vermicelli-Rice-Noodles/" title="Chang's Vermicelli Rice  Noodles" alt="Chang's Vermicelli Rice  Noodles">Chang's Vermicelli Rice  Noodles</a> according to the packet instructions.</li>
  <li><strong> Assemble the bowls.<br /></strong>Add a ladle of fresh tomato butter sauce to each bowl and top with a bundle of <a href="/products/Changs-Vermicelli-Rice-Noodles/" title="Chang's Vermicelli Rice  Noodles" alt="Chang's Vermicelli Rice  Noodles">Chang's Vermicelli Rice  Noodles</a>.<br />Spoon hot caramelised pork mince over the top and finish with shredded cucumber, fresh coriander and spring onion.<br /><br />Mix well and enjoy!</li>
  </ol>
